## Executive Summary

### Research Scope

This document presents research findings on funding opportunities, grants, and financial support available for DeLimerence -- an anti-dependency conversational AI tool for limerence recovery. The research covers UK government grants, charitable foundations, impact investors, accelerators, EU/international funding, regulatory sandbox programmes, and grant discovery platforms. This is a major scope expansion from v1.0 which covered technology and infrastructure research.

**Research Categories Identified**: 6 categories based on project positioning at the intersection of mental health technology, AI safety, responsible AI, and online harms prevention

**Research Approach**: Systematic web research across UK government funding portals (UKRI, NIHR, DSIT, AISI), charitable foundations (Wellcome Trust, Medical Research Foundation), impact investors (KHP Ventures, BGV, Zinc), EU programmes (Horizon Europe), regulatory bodies (MHRA AI Airlock), and grant aggregation platforms (360Giving, GrantNav, GOV.UK Find a Grant).

### Key Findings

- **Over 30 funding sources identified** across 6 categories, with at least 12 currently accepting or soon to open applications
- **Strongest alignment**: AISI Systemic Safety Grants (AI safety + mental health intersection), Wellcome Trust Generative AI programme (digital mental health), MHRA/NICE AI Airlock (regulatory sandbox with Wellcome funding), and Innovate UK Innovation Funding Service competitions
- **Gap identified**: Most mental health funding targets depression, anxiety, and psychosis -- limerence is not explicitly mentioned in any programme. DeLimerence must frame applications around the clinical evidence (OCD-adjacent, CBT/ERP protocols) and the online harms / AI safety angle
- **Estimated accessible funding range**: GBP 50,000 - 3,000,000 depending on programme, partnership structure, and application positioning
- **Critical requirement**: Several high-value programmes (NIHR, Wellcome, ESRC) require academic partnership -- the founder should prioritise establishing a university research collaboration
- **Year 1 costs (GBP 75,000 - 160,000) are within range** for multiple programmes: AISI fast grants (up to GBP 200,000), SBRI Healthcare (up to GBP 200,000), BGV (GBP 60,000 + equity), Innovate UK competitions (GBP 25,000 - 500,000), UnLtd awards (up to GBP 18,000)

### Funding Strategy Recommendation
